# INFO 4401 (FYP 1) Report Writing Guidelines

This document compiles the guidelines for writing Chapters 1, 2, and 3 of the Final Year Project (FYP 1) report for INFO 4401, based on the provided seminar slides.

---

## Chapter 1: Introduction

### 1.0 Preamble
* States the reasons for and intent of the document.
* Provide an overview of your report.
* Define the topic/key terms (e.g., Project name: iHeart - a heart rate monitoring mobile application utilizing a heart rate band, specifically designed for the elderly).
* Explain the value of your study.

### 1.1 Problem Description
* **1.1.1 Background of the problem:** Clear description of the issue.
* **1.1.2 Problem statement:** Use the 5Ws (What? When? Why? Who? Where?) to spark discussions.
    * Who will be affected?
    * Why is it important to be fixed?
    * What will be the outcome if it is not fixed?
    * Where is the problem taking place?
    * When does the problem need to be fixed?
* *Note: Avoid writing generic or purely experiential problem statements. Be specific about the missing solutions and the consequences.*

### 1.2 Project Objectives
Project objectives must be **SMART**:
* **S** - Specific (What do you want to accomplish?)
* **M** - Measurable (How can you measure progress?)
* **A** - Achievable (Do you have the skills required?)
* **R** - Relevant (Why am I setting this goal right now?)
* **T** - Time-bound (What's the deadline? Is it realistic?)

### 1.3 Project Scope
* **1.3.1 Scope:** Your coverage (e.g., specific views or features like Parent View vs. Caregiver View).
* **1.3.2 Target Audience:** Define who the system is for (e.g., elderly people aged 50-70 and their caregivers).
* **1.3.3 Specific Platform:** Define the tools (e.g., React Native for Android/iOS, or specific software like Microsoft Windows 8, Adobe Photoshop CS 6, etc.).

### 1.4 Constraints
Identify constraints affecting your project. The main constraints include:
* Scope
* Cost
* Quality
* Time (e.g., time taken to learn new knowledge or programming languages)
* Risk
* Resources

### 1.5 Project Stages
* The timeline of your project (e.g., Gantt Chart showing stages like First Presentation, Prototype, Final Presentation, etc.).

### 1.6 Significance of the Project
Explain the benefits of the project. Common approaches include:
* Increasing understanding or knowledge in a certain area.
* Improving organizations' services.
* Contributing to organization policy.
* Explaining benefits of the project or who likely benefits from it.

### 1.7 Project Summary
A concise summary covering the Problem, Objectives, Significance, Scope, and Constraints of the project.

## Chapter 2: Literature Review

### 2.0 Introduction
A comprehensive survey and critical analysis of existing published work (or existing projects/products).
* Provide the context for the research/project.
* Justify the research/project.
* Identify what research has been done before.
* Highlight what aspects of your research/project are beneficial.

### 2.1 Analytical Method
Specify your analysis methods for the review.
* **SWOT:** Evaluates internal Strengths/Weaknesses and external Opportunities/Threats.
* **Function Analysis:** Evaluates what each component does and how they work together.
* **Technical Requirements:** Evaluates standards (electrical, mechanical, ergonomic).
* **Manufacturing Method:** Analyzes why specific processes/materials were chosen.
* **Improvement Assessment:** Identifies alternative designs to improve functionality.

### 2.1.1 Existing Products (System / Multimedia Review)
* Discuss at least three (3) existing products on the market or within an organization.
* Provide detailed screenshots and developer information (include URLs if available).
* Use APA citation format (e.g., Developer/Company, Year of release).
* **2.1.2 Advantages:** Discuss and compare the advantages of the selected products in detail with relevant screenshots.
* **2.1.3 Disadvantages:** Discuss and compare the disadvantages of the selected products in detail with relevant screenshots.

### 2.2 System / Multimedia Adaptation
* Based on the advantages and disadvantages of existing products, describe which existing features and improvements you want to include in your project.

### 2.3 Discussions
* Summarize the aspects that have been reviewed.
* Use a table comparing the advantages and disadvantages of the reviewed research/products.
* Address the lessons that inspired your project.

### Guidelines for Writing the Literature Review
* Write mostly in your own words.
* Acknowledge contributions using Quotations (use selectively, mainly for statistics, definitions, or expert opinions) and Paraphrasing.
* Use tools for discovery (Consensus, Elicit, Semantic Scholar), mapping (Paperguide, SciSpace), and writing (Paperpal, QuillBot).

## Chapter 3: Methodology

This chapter comprehensively describes and justifies all the methodological choices made in your study/project.

### 3.1 Development Approach
State in detail the development approach used to develop the system and justify why it was chosen.
* **Waterfall Model:** Linear and sequential; best for projects with well-defined, unchanging requirements.
* **Iterative Model:** Repeated cycles (iterations) that progressively enhance functionality.
* **Spiral Model:** Combines iterative development with focus on risk management.
* **Agile Model:** Iterative, flexible, focuses on delivering small increments in short cycles (sprints) with continuous feedback.
* **Rapid Prototyping:** Quick creation of preliminary versions to test functionality and usability.
* **ADDIE Model (For E-Learning):** Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, and Evaluation.
* **Game Design Stages:** Preproduction (foundation, character/story), Production (mechanics, animation), Postproduction (testing/QA).

### 3.2 Requirements Specifications / Data Collection
Choose suitable data collection methods based on research objectives, data type (qualitative/quantitative), and resources.
* Survey questionnaires
* End-user/system user interviews
* Forms documentation
* Existing system/database research
* Observation / Site visits

### 3.3 Design and Diagram

#### For System Development:
* **Logical Design:** Use Case Diagrams & Narratives, Activity Diagrams, Sequence Diagrams, System Architecture, Class/Package Diagrams.
* **Database Design:** Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) and Data Dictionary/Metadata for tables.
* **User Interface/Prototype Design:** Detailed navigation or page flow (dialogue chart) and screen layout designs.

#### For Digital Storytelling and Game Development:
* **Conceptual Design:** Story plot/scripts, Role of characters.
* **Character Design:** Detailed descriptions and model sheets/sketches for protagonists and antagonists.
* **Storyboard:** Scene-by-scene descriptions with drawn scenes, dialogue, and special/multimedia effects.

#### For E-Learning Applications:
* **Content Development:** Writing or collecting required knowledge/information (e.g., content outlines).
* **Navigation Flow:** Page flow of the application from start to finish.
* **Storyboard:** Integrating instructional methods and media elements (images, text, interactions, assessments).
