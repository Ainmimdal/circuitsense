# Writing Style Rules

Apply every rule below to every sentence written or rewritten for this project.

---

## Rules

**1. No em dashes.**
Replace with a comma, a full stop, or rephrase the sentence.

**2. No semicolons.**
Break into two sentences or use a comma instead.

**3. No "it is X, not Y" constructions.**
Say what something IS. Do not define it by contrast with what it is not.

**4. No filler openers.**
Do not start sentences with: "it is worth noting", "it is important to", "this is significant because", "in this context". Say the thing directly.

**5. No stacked adjectives before nouns.**
Pick one adjective or none. Do not write "a robust, scalable, extensible framework".

**6. Write like a Malaysian IT undergraduate.**
- Straightforward sentence structure, not literary.
- Slightly formal but not stiff — the register of a KICT lab report.
- Passive voice is acceptable and natural in methodology sections.
- "The system" or "the proposed system" is fine as an alternative to the project name.

**7. Banned words — never use any of these:**
delve, leverage, robust, seamlessly, harness, streamline, cutting-edge, transformative, paradigm, utilize

**8. Every factual claim about an external tool or study needs a citation.**
Use IEEE numbered format: [1], [2], etc.

**9. Do not invent citations.**
If a source is missing, write [CITATION NEEDED] in the text.

---

## Citation Format

**Journal / conference paper:**
A. Author and B. Author, "Title of paper," *Journal or Conference Name*, vol. X, no. X, pp. XX–XX, Year.

**Website / online source:**
Organisation, "Page title," Site Name. [Online]. Available: URL. [Accessed: Month Year].

---

## Quick Checklist Before Submitting Any Writing

- [ ] No em dashes in the entire text
- [ ] No semicolons in the entire text
- [ ] No banned words
- [ ] No filler openers
- [ ] Every external claim has a [X] citation or [CITATION NEEDED]
- [ ] Lists of adjectives before nouns removed
- [ ] Tone matches a Malaysian undergraduate IT report
